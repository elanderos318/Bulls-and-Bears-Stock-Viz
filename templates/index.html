Bulls & Bears

Visualization of Capital Markets


The first chart displays S&P 500 prices from Jan 3, 2007 to Mar 25, 2019. The blue lines represent "bull" markets, whereas the red lines represent "bear markets". The market changes when prices rise above or fall below a certain threshold, usually 20%, from all-time lows and all-time highs respectively. The last 12 or so years have been defined by a sharp rise in index value, one of the strongest bull markets in history.

The three histograms below the S&P 500 represent the distribution of 'absolute' daily price changes in the index - the first over the entire range of data, the second only during bull markets, and the third only during bear markets. These help provide a picture of 'volatility' during times of boom and bust. Below, you can adjust the sensitivity of what defines a 'market change' (default 20).
